In Vim search patterns, what does * mean?	The previous item matches zero or more times.
Why can /a* match the empty string?	Because * allows zero occurrences.
In the pattern /ab*/, what does * apply to?	Only the b.
How do you repeat a multi-character group in Vim regex?	Wrap it in \( ... \) before adding a quantifier.
Pattern for repeating "ab" zero or more times	/\(ab\)*
In Vim regex, what does \+ mean?	One or more occurrences of the previous item.
Does /ab\+/ match "a" with no trailing b?	No.
In Vim regex, what does \= mean?	Zero or one occurrence (optional).
What does /folders\= match?	Both "folder" and "folders".
What does \{n,m} specify in Vim regex?	Match the previous item from n to m times (inclusive).
Are \{n,m} bounds inclusive?	Yes.
If n is omitted in \{,m}, what is the default?	0.
If m is omitted in \{n,}, what is the default?	Infinity (no upper limit).
What does \{n} mean?	Exactly n matches.
What is \{0,1} equivalent to?	\=
What is \{0,} equivalent to?	*
What is \{1,} equivalent to?	\+
Minimal-match count form in Vim regex	\{-n,m}
What does \{-} mean?	Zero or more matches, as few as possible.
On "axbxb", what does /a.\{-}b match?	axb
On "axbxb", what does /a.*b match?	axbxb
What is the alternation operator in Vim regex?	\|
What does /one\|two\|three match?	one, two, or three.
How do you apply a quantifier to alternatives like foo|bar?	Group them with \( ... \), then apply the quantifier.
What does /\(foo\|bar\)\+ match?	One or more consecutive "foo"/"bar" chunks.
What does /end\(if\|while\|for\) match?	endif, endwhile, and endfor.
What does \& require in a pattern?	Both sub-patterns must match at the same position.
With \&, which alternative determines the returned match text?	The last alternative.
What does /forever\&... match in "forever"?	for
What does a [] range match?	A single character from the set/range.
What does /[0-9a-f]/ match?	One character that is 0-9 or a-f.
How do you match a literal - inside []?	Put - first or last in the range.
What does ^ mean at the start of []?	Complement the set (match any character not listed).
What does /"[^"]*"/ match?	A double-quoted string on one line, without inner double quotes.
What is \a equivalent to?	[a-zA-Z]
What is \d equivalent to?	[0-9]
What is \x equivalent to?	[0-9a-fA-F]
Can predefined ranges like \d and \l be used inside []?	No.
How do you match "digit or lowercase alpha" using predefined ranges?	\(\d\|\l\)
Predefined ranges vs explicit [] ranges in Vim search speed	Predefined ranges are faster.
What does \i match?	Identifier characters (defined by 'isident').
What does \k match?	Keyword characters (defined by 'iskeyword').
What does \p match?	Printable characters (defined by 'isprint').
What does \n match in a Vim search pattern?	A line break at that position.
What does /one\ntwo match?	"one" at end of a line followed by "two" at start of next line.
What does \_s match?	Whitespace or a line break.
What does /one\_s\+two allow between "one" and "two"?	Any amount of whitespace, including line breaks.
Difference between \s and \_s	\s matches whitespace; \_s matches whitespace or a line break.
What does prefixing an item with \_ do?	Makes that item also match line breaks.
What does \_. match?	Any character, including a line break.
Why be careful with \_.*?	It can match until end-of-file and make searches very slow.
What is \_[]?	A character range that also includes line breaks.
What does /"\_[^"]*"/ find?	Double-quoted text that can span multiple lines.
