
It is important to structure your time in order to **protect your attention and provide your brain with clear stopping points**.

This is because:
1. It creates boundaries
	- Instead of “I might check things”, it becomes “I check once in the morning”.
2. It reduces decision fatigue. 
	- If you have to constantly decide “what should I do now?”, your brain stays in a low-level stressed state.
3. It allows real rest
	- If your time isn’t structured, “rest” becomes accidental scrolling or stimulation.
### Structure should be simple and coarse, not rigid.

**You don’t need a minute-by-minute schedule. That often backfires.**
